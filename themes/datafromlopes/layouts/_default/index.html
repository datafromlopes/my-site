Software Engineer, Data with years building large-scale distributed systems in production — and an MSc researcher at USP studying how language models can understand and interact with complex computational systems. My engineering work lives at the boundary of distributed systems, high-throughput data platforms, and low-level performance. My research asks a harder question: can a model read a scientific paper and reproduce the experiment described in it? I write about systems engineering, NLP research, and what happens when the two collide.

Beyond the Code

When I'm not architecting data pipelines or fine-tuning language models, you'll find me in the cockpit pursuing my second passion: aviation.

There's something deeply satisfying about the parallels between flying and engineering — both require meticulous planning, precise execution, and the ability to adapt when conditions change. Whether I'm debugging a distributed system at 30,000 feet of altitude or optimizing query performance on the ground, the challenge is what drives me.

Flying has taught me to stay calm under pressure, make critical decisions with incomplete information, and always have a backup plan. Skills that translate surprisingly well to production systems running at scale.
